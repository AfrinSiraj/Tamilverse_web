Fort St. George – Chennai

Fort St. George is the first English fortress built in India and stands as a landmark of British colonial history in Chennai.

Constructed in 1644 by the British East India Company, the fort served as the nucleus for the city of Madras (now Chennai).

The fort complex houses the Fort Museum, St. Mary’s Church (the oldest Anglican church in India), and important administrative buildings.

Today, Fort St. George functions as the administrative headquarters of the Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly and Secretariat.
