Padmanabhapuram Palace – Kanyakumari

Padmanabhapuram Palace is a magnificent wooden palace located near Kanyakumari in Tamil Nadu. It served as the former capital of the Travancore kingdom.

Built in the traditional Kerala architectural style, the palace is famous for its intricate wooden carvings, sloping roofs, and exquisite craftsmanship.

The palace complex consists of several structures including the council chamber, clock tower, secret passages, and murals depicting mythological stories.

Maintained by the Archaeological Department of Kerala, the palace is considered one of the largest and best-preserved wooden palaces in India.
