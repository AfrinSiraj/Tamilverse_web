Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace – Madurai

Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace is a magnificent 17th-century palace located in Madurai, Tamil Nadu. It was built in 1636 by King Thirumalai Nayak of the Nayak dynasty.

The palace is renowned for its grand Dravidian–Islamic architectural style, featuring massive pillars, wide arches, and a majestic central courtyard.

Originally four times larger than its present size, the palace once served as the royal residence and administrative center of the Nayak rulers.

Today, the palace is a protected monument and hosts a popular sound and light show that narrates the history of Madurai and the Nayak dynasty.
