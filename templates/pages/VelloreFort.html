Vellore Fort – Vellore

Vellore Fort is a magnificent 16th-century fort located in Vellore city, Tamil Nadu. It is one of the finest examples of South Indian military architecture.

Built by the Vijayanagara rulers, the fort is known for its massive granite walls, deep moat, and robust defensive structures.

Inside the fort complex lies the historic Jalakandeswarar Temple, along with a mosque and a church, symbolizing religious harmony.

Vellore Fort is also historically significant for the Vellore Mutiny of 1806, one of the earliest uprisings against British rule in India.
